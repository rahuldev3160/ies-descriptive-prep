# RBI DEPR 2026 — Source Document 2: Indian Economy & Fiscal Policy

## 1. GDP Growth & National Accounts

**Real GDP Growth:** The Economic Survey 2025-26 projects India's GDP growth at approximately **7.6% in FY26E** (Advance Estimate). The government has estimated nominal GDP growth of **10% in 2026-27** (real growth + inflation).

**Key Drivers:** India's growth is supported by:
- Strong domestic demand (reflected in UPI transactions, passenger vehicle sales, air passenger data)
- Capital expenditure push by the central government
- Services sector resilience (services trade surplus supports the current account)
- Moderation in inflation improving real purchasing power

**GVA (Gross Value Added):** GVA differs from GDP by excluding taxes on products and adding subsidies. Growth in manufacturing GVA deflator has remained moderate, and the share of manufacturing in real GVA has stayed stable. Sectoral growth in H1 FY26 was higher than pre-pandemic trends.

**Sectoral Breakdown:**
- Agriculture and allied activities: contribution to GDP is approximately 16–18%; supported by normal monsoon and horticulture expansion.
- Industry (manufacturing + mining + electricity): approximately 26–28% of GVA.
- Services: approximately 55% of GVA; the largest and most dynamic sector.

## 2. Inflation

**CPI (Consumer Price Index):** The headline CPI inflation in FY26 has moderated significantly, tracking towards the 4% target. Monthly CPI components — headline, core, and food — show declining trends.

**Food inflation** has been a key driver historically; measures to reduce pulses and vegetable price volatility are underway.

**Core inflation** (excluding food and fuel) shows base and momentum effects. CPI trends for vegetables, pulses, and spices have been closely tracked.

**WPI (Wholesale Price Index):** The WPI is under review for replacement by PPI (Producer Price Index). Criticisms of WPI: (a) double/multiple counting; (b) excludes exports and imports; (c) excludes services (55% of GDP). The government has been asked to suggest a roadmap for switching from WPI to PPI.

**IIP (Index of Industrial Production):** Measures output in manufacturing, mining, and electricity. Monthly data used for tracking industrial momentum.

## 3. Fiscal Position — Centre

### Fiscal Deficit
- **FY25 (Actual):** The fiscal deficit was achieved at **4.8% of GDP**, better than the budgeted 4.9%.
- **FY26 (RE/Target):** Fiscal deficit target is **4.4% of GDP**.
- **FY27 (BE):** Fiscal deficit targeted at **4.3% of GDP**.
- **Long-run goal:** Reduce the Union fiscal deficit by more than half from its COVID peak — a promise made in 2021. The FRBM Act, 2003 requires progressive reduction in outstanding debt, revenue deficit, and fiscal deficit with three-year rolling targets.

### Revenue Deficit
- **FY26 RE:** Revenue deficit at approximately **1.5% of GDP**.
- **FY27 BE:** Revenue deficit targeted at **1.5% of GDP**.
- Revenue deficit = Total revenue expenditure − Total revenue receipts. A persistent revenue deficit means the government borrows to fund current consumption, crowding out capital formation.

### Primary Deficit
- Primary deficit = Fiscal deficit − Net interest payments.
- India's primary deficit has been declining as a share of GDP.
- Global context: Primary deficits in major economies are higher than pre-pandemic levels (per BIS data, January 2026).

### Effective Revenue Deficit
- Effective Revenue Deficit (ERD) = Revenue Deficit − Grants for creation of capital assets.
- ERD is lower than the simple revenue deficit, as capital grants transferred to states are netted out.

### Deficit Trend (from Budget documents):
| Indicator | FY22-23 | FY25-26 RE | FY26-27 BE |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fiscal Deficit (% GDP) | 6.4% | 4.4% | 4.3% |
| Revenue Deficit (% GDP) | 3.00 | 1.50 | 1.50 |
| Primary Deficit (% GDP) | 2.80 | 0.70 | 0.30 |

## 4. Union Budget 2026-27: Key Numbers

- **Total Expenditure:** Rs 53,47,315 crore in 2026-27 — **7.7% higher** than RE of 2025-26.
- **Interest Payments:** Account for **26% of total expenditure** and **40% of revenue receipts**.
- **Total Receipts (excl. borrowings):** Rs 36,51,547 crore — 7.2% higher than RE 2025-26.
- **Tax Revenue:** Expected to increase by **8%** over RE for 2025-26.
- **Outstanding Liabilities:** 55.6% of GDP in 2026-27. Target: reduce to approximately 50% of GDP by March 2031.

### Capital Expenditure
- **Focus on Capex:** The government has consistently emphasised capital expenditure over revenue transfers to avoid crowding out private investment and improve multiplier effects.
- Average annual construction capex on Roads and Highways has risen substantially.
- Railway network commissioning per year and capital expenditure on railways have increased.
- SASCI (Special Assistance to States for Capital Expenditure/Investment) incentivises states to maintain capex.

### Disinvestment
- FY25-26 disinvestment target: Rs 47,000 crore. Government met approximately **71.9%** of this target.
- FY26-27 disinvestment target: **Rs 80,000 crore** — the first increase in the disinvestment target after five consecutive years of downward revisions.
- Total disinvestment receipts in FY24-25 (actual): Rs 33,837 crore.
- FY22-23 actual: Rs 17,202 crore; FY23-24 BE was Rs 51,000 crore but revised down.
- Disinvestment is the government selling its stakes in Public Sector Undertakings (PSUs).

## 5. Tax Reforms (Budget 2026-27)

- **No change in income tax slabs** for AY 2026-27.
- **Tax holiday until 2047** for foreign companies providing global cloud services using Indian data centres.
- **IFSC tax holiday** extended from 10 to 20 years; income taxed at 15% thereafter.
- **Share buybacks:** All share buybacks to be taxed as capital gains; effective rate 22% for corporate, 30% for non-corporate promoters.
- **STT increase:** From 0.1% to 0.15% on options; from 0.02% to 0.05% on futures.
- **MAT reduction:** Rate reduced from 15% to 14%; no credit accumulation from April 1, 2026.
- **TCS reduction:** On education/medical remittances above Rs 10 lakh — reduced from 5% to 2%.
- **Income Tax Act 2025:** New codification; various simplification and decriminalisation measures.

## 6. External Sector

### Current Account
- **Current Account Deficit (CAD):** Remained moderate at **0.8% of GDP** in H1 FY26.
- India runs a trade deficit in goods. Net trade surplus in services and remittances partially offsets it. Dependence on foreign capital inflows for rupee stability is a structural challenge.

### Forex Reserves
- Forex reserves cover over **11 months of imports** (as of January 16, 2026).
- Cover approximately **94%** of external debt outstanding (September 2025).
- Gold reserves: **$102.3 billion** — an all-time high.

### Exchange Rate
- The Indian rupee underperformed in 2025, punching below its weight given India's strong fundamentals.
- Rupee valuation does not fully reflect India's strong GDP growth, moderate inflation, and improving current account.

### Trade Policy Context
- US imposed an additional penal tariff of 25% on most Indian merchandise exports (on top of the reciprocal tariff).
- India needs to generate sufficient export earnings to cover rising import bills.
- India's long-run challenge: generate sustained external surpluses through exports, productivity, and financial depth.

## 7. Financial Inclusion

### PM Jan Dhan Yojana (PMJDY)
- Nearly **56 crore beneficiaries** under PM Jan Dhan Yojana.
- Financial Inclusion Index rose to **67 in 2025**, a 24.3% increase — reflects broader access to credit for vulnerable groups.
- Overdraft facility: Rs 10,000 sanctioned under PMJDY accounts (also classified as MUDRA loans).

### MUDRA (Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency) — PM Mudra Yojana (PMMY)
- Collateral-free loans for micro-enterprises.
- Categories: Shishu (up to Rs 50,000), Kishore (Rs 50,001 – Rs 5 lakh), Tarun (Rs 5 lakh – Rs 10 lakh).
- No collateral security or guarantor required.
- PMJDY overdraft of Rs 10,000 is classified under MUDRA/PMMY.

### Credit-Deposit (CD) Ratio
- Indicates what share of deposits is deployed as credit.
- Higher CD ratio = higher credit growth but lower liquidity buffer.
- Lower CD ratio = conservative lending, higher liquidity, possibly inefficient deposit use.

### UPI as Financial Inclusion Metric
- UPI transaction data is used in the Economic Survey's nowcasting model as an indicator of real-time economic activity.
- Reflects penetration of digital payments across income groups.
