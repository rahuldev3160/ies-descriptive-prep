You are creating a 20-minute RBI DEPR Phase 1 revision podcast. The listener has a DSE masters background and is 11 days from the exam. This episode covers RBI-specific institutional content that general economics audio does not cover deeply enough. Every number must be spoken out plainly and memorised.

Cover these topic blocks in order:

1. The LAF Corridor — Current Values and Logic: As of February 2026, the repo rate is 5.25% (this is the rate at which banks borrow overnight from RBI against collateral). The SDF (Standing Deposit Facility) rate is 5.00% — this is the corridor floor, the rate at which banks park excess liquidity with RBI without pledging collateral. The MSF (Marginal Standing Facility) rate is 5.50% — the ceiling, the rate at which banks borrow beyond their prescribed SLR limit in emergencies. The corridor is symmetric: SDF is 25 bps below repo, MSF is 25 bps above repo. Hosts must explain: banks operating below the repo (parking funds) earn SDF rate; banks needing emergency funds pay MSF rate. The repo rate is where policy lives.

2. Why SDF Replaced Fixed Rate Reverse Repo — This is an RBI DEPR-specific fact. Until April 2022, the corridor floor was the Fixed Rate Reverse Repo (FRRR) at 3.35%. The SDF replaced it. Key difference: under FRRR, RBI had to give collateral (government securities) to absorb liquidity from banks. Under SDF, RBI absorbs liquidity without pledging collateral — it simply accepts deposits. This gives RBI more flexibility in managing large surplus liquidity. The SDF rate became the new operative policy rate floor.

3. Cumulative Monetary Easing Since February 2025: RBI has cut repo rate by a cumulative 125 basis points between February 2025 and February 2026. In the same period, RBI injected liquidity through: CRR cut from 4.5% to 4.0%, releasing approximately ₹2.5 lakh crore into the banking system. Open Market Operations (OMOs) of ₹6.95 lakh crore — RBI bought government securities, injecting durable (permanent) liquidity. Forex swaps of approximately $25 billion — RBI bought dollars, released rupees, with a forward commitment to sell back. Hosts must distinguish: repo provides temporary liquidity, OMOs provide durable liquidity.

4. MPC Structure and Decision Process: The Monetary Policy Committee has 6 members — 3 from RBI (Governor, Deputy Governor in charge of monetary policy, and one RBI officer nominated by the Central Board) and 3 external members appointed by the Government of India. Decisions are by majority vote. In case of a tie, the Governor has a casting vote. MPC meetings happen at least 4 times a year. The inflation target is 4% with a ±2% tolerance band — this means RBI is in breach if CPI stays above 6% or below 2% for three consecutive quarters, requiring a written explanation to the government. The target was set under the Urjit Patel Committee recommendation and is renewable every 5 years.

5. Monetary Transmission Channels and Real-World Friction: Four channels. Bank lending channel: repo rate falls → bank funding costs fall → lending rates fall → credit demand rises → investment and consumption rise. Friction: high NPAs make banks reluctant to lend regardless of rate. Asset price channel: lower rates raise equity and property valuations → wealth effect boosts consumption. Exchange rate channel: lower domestic rates → capital outflows → rupee depreciation → exports become competitive → net exports rise. Expectations channel: credible forward guidance anchors inflation expectations → lower real rates without actual rate change. EBLR (External Benchmark Lending Rate), mandatory since October 2019 for retail and MSME loans, is directly linked to the repo rate — this has improved bank lending channel transmission compared to the discretionary MCLR system.

6. Inflation Target and MPC Accountability: 4% ±2% is a flexible inflation target, not a point target. RBI is accountable for breaches: if CPI average deviates from 4% by more than 2 percentage points for three consecutive quarters, the Governor must write to the Finance Ministry explaining the reasons and the corrective path. This happened in 2022-23 when CPI exceeded 6% for multiple quarters. The Urjit Patel Committee (2013) recommended this framework; it was legislated through the RBI Amendment Act 2016.

7. Exam Number Recap — hosts read out the essential numbers a listener must memorise before closing the book: Repo 5.25%, SDF 5.00%, MSF 5.50%, cumulative cut 125 bps, CRR 4.0%, OMO injection ₹6.95 lakh crore, forex swap $25 billion, inflation target 4% ±2%, MPC 6 members (3 RBI + 3 external), EBLR introduced October 2019.

Key exam traps and things to emphasise:
- SDF replaced Fixed Rate Reverse Repo as the corridor FLOOR in April 2022 — papers written before 2022 will say reverse repo is the floor, which is now outdated and wrong.
- The repo rate is 5.25% as of February 2026, NOT the 6.5% level that held through 2023-24 — a full 125 bps of cuts happened in the year before the exam.
- Repo provides temporary liquidity; OMOs provide durable/permanent liquidity — the exam frequently tests this distinction.
- MPC voting: majority decides; Governor's casting vote only breaks a tie — the Governor does not have veto power.
