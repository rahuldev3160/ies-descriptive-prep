Focus exclusively on Monetary Policy and Banking in India — for RBI DEPR Phase 1, June 14, 2026. This is a 15-minute deep-dive revision audio.

Cover these specific topics in sequence:

1. RBI's Monetary Policy Framework: MPC structure (6-member composition — 3 RBI, 3 external), inflation target of 4% ±2% (Urjit Patel Committee recommendation), the 5-year mandate review mechanism, and the February 2026 MPC decision to hold repo rate at 5.25% after a cumulative 125 bps cut since February 2025.

2. The LAF Corridor: Repo rate (5.25%) as the borrowing anchor, SDF as the corridor floor replacing Fixed Rate Reverse Repo (no collateral), MSF as the ceiling. Why the SDF change matters: RBI absorbs liquidity without pledging collateral, giving it more flexibility. Explain the corridor logic — how banks park excess funds or borrow depending on their position.

3. Reserve Requirements and OMOs: CRR as a non-interest-bearing reserve (₹2.5 lakh crore injected via CRR cuts in FY25-26); SLR as a liquid-assets buffer that earns returns; OMO as a durable liquidity tool (₹6.95 lakh crore injected); forex swaps (~$25 billion). Contrast: repo is temporary liquidity, OMO is permanent.

4. Monetary Transmission: The chain from repo rate cut → call money rate → MCLR/external benchmark lending rate → credit demand → investment → output. Explain External Benchmark Lending Rate (EBLR) introduced October 2019 and why it speeds up transmission compared to older MCLR. Mention that declining sovereign 10-year yields act as a fiscal stimulus.

5. Banking Health — CRAR and NPA: CRAR norm of 9% per RBI (above Basel III 8%); GNPA ratio at approximately 2.6% (multi-year low in FY26); IBC (Insolvency and Bankruptcy Code) as the resolution mechanism that drove NPA improvement. Brief on PCR (Provisioning Coverage Ratio) as the buffer indicator.

6. Priority Sector Lending: 40% of ANBC must go to priority sectors. List the categories: Agriculture, MSMEs, Export Credit, Education, Housing, Social Infrastructure, Renewable Energy, Weaker Sections. Co-lending model (banks + NBFCs jointly originate). Credit-deposit ratio interpretation.

7. Payment Systems Architecture: UPI (NPCI, real-time, used as a nowcasting indicator in Economic Survey), RTGS (high-value, real-time final settlement), NEFT (batch-mode, 24x7 since December 2019), IMPS (immediate, retail, 24x7). Why this matters for RBI DEPR: financial inclusion section.

8. Exam-relevant numbers to memorise: Repo 5.25%, 125 bps cut, CRR injection ₹2.5 lakh crore, OMO ₹6.95 lakh crore, GNPA 2.6%, CRAR 9%, PSL 40%, PMJDY 56 crore beneficiaries, Financial Inclusion Index 67 (2025), Forex reserves >11 months import cover, Gold $102.3 billion.

DO NOT cover: fiscal policy, GDP growth, budget numbers (that is RBI-A2); international trade theory; microeconomic theory; state finance.

For every number or ratio: give it in plain spoken English first, then explain what it means economically. Example: "The GNPA ratio is two point six percent as of FY26 — meaning for every hundred rupees banks have lent out, two rupees sixty paise is classified as non-performing, which is the lowest this number has been in many years."
